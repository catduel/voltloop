# Bury It! Safety and Anonymity

## Core Rule

No one should be identifiable through a public burial.

## Product Promise

`Bury It!` is built around complete public anonymity. Users do not provide first names, last names, profile photos, or public identity markers.

## V1 Anonymity Requirements

- No image uploads
- No real-name fields
- No public usernames chosen by users
- Use generated anonymous handles only if needed internally
- Strip metadata from stored content
- Block or flag emails, phone numbers, links, exact addresses, and account IDs in public submissions

## Public Burial Rules

Public burials must not contain:

- full names
- phone numbers
- email addresses
- social handles
- workplace identifiers
- school identifiers
- home addresses
- threats
- harassment
- revenge content
- explicit self-harm instructions

## Moderation Approach

### Automated Checks

- detect sensitive identifiers
- detect abusive language
- detect high-risk self-harm language
- detect targeted harassment patterns

### Product Actions

- warn before posting
- force private-only save for risky entries
- send edge cases to moderation review
- remove public burials that violate policy

## Recommended Public Safety UX

- Show a privacy warning before public burial
- Highlight risky phrases before submission
- Suggest rewriting instead of blocking without explanation
- Default uncertain content to private

## Tone Rules

The product should feel:

- respectful
- calm
- emotionally safe
- non-performative

The product should avoid:

- mockery
- meme-heavy reactions
- aggressive gamification
- sensational presentation of pain

## Why This Matters

The app becomes more trusted when users believe:

- they cannot be exposed
- they cannot accidentally expose others
- the public graveyard is not a cruelty machine

That trust is the foundation of retention.
